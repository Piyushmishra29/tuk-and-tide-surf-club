The Club

We're Tuk & Tide.

We're a little surf club on Weligama's south-coast sand — the kind of place where you grab a board for the waves and a tuk for the road, and we sort out the rest.

Lessons for first-timers, rentals for the ones who already know the drill, tuk-tuk tours out to the good stuff and three-wheelers to borrow when you fancy your own adventure. Everything here is hand-run by locals who've grown up reading these breaks and these back-roads — no clipboards, no rush, no nonsense. Just salt in the air, sand on the floor and someone who'll point you at the right wave. Roll in a stranger, leave with a story and a tan. That's the whole plan.

The South
Coast

our island, hand-painted
Twenty kilometres of warm reef, point and beach-break, strung between palm and paddy along the deep south. Pick a wave, flag a three-wheeler, and chase the swell.
1Weligama Bay
Soft, forgiving sand-bottom rollers — where almost everyone stands up for the first time. Our home water.
2Mirissa
Mornings for blue whales offshore; evenings for the longest, reddest sunsets on the coast.
3Midigama
The Lazy Left and Ram’s Right — shallow reef, quick walls, for when you’re ready to graduate.
4Ahangama
Long, peeling points that keep going well past breakfast. Patience and a good paddle.
Boards on the roof-rack, salt in your hair — the tuk-tuk is simply how you get from one break to the next.

Logo Don'ts

The mark is hand-carved and sun-faded — treat it that way. Six quick ways to wreck it. Don't.

✗
Don't stretch it.
Never squash or stretch the mark. Keep it locked to its original proportions.
✗
Don't tilt it.
No rotating, angling or jaunty tilts. The tuk rolls level, always.
✗
Don't go off-brand.
No clashing backgrounds or recoloured ink. Stick to the five heritage tones.
✗
Don't add effects.
No glows, drop-shadows, outlines or gradients. The screenprint is flat by design.
